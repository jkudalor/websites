Springland International School Celebrates AU day

By Michael Amegbe -Admin The 25TH of May, 1963 shall forever remain a turning point in the political history of Africa. This was the day the Organization of African Unity (O.A.U) was founded by a few selfless and visionary African Leaders. Prominent among the Founding Fathers was Dr. Kwame Nkrumah, President of Ghana; who on the eve of Ghana’s Independence, openly declared, “The independence of Ghana is meaningless, unless it is linked up with total liberation of Africa.”

The birth of O.A.U. did not only give hope to the ordinary African for a better tomorrow; but also assured all Africans of the realization of Kwame Nkrumah’s vision of total emancipation of the African continent. Today, it is worthy to note that only a very insignificant number of African countries are yet to attain independence. In order to immortalize the work of our leaders, O.A.U., which is now known as “African Union,” has set aside 25th May every year, for people all over the world, particularly Africans and people of African descent to commemorate the day and celebrate our leaders as well as our freedom.

To us as a school, we believe African Union Day, which is also known as Africa Day, bestows on us the responsibility of nurturing our children in an environment, whereby they will grow up to appreciate the works of our past and present leaders. In this way, they will be positioned to aspire and build upon the efforts of our past heroes and heroines; thereby making our country, Ghana, a better place for all to live.

Gist SIS celebrates AU day. A milestone for Ghana and the Continent at whole and the hope of a brighter future.
